The KQL Query Builder Redesign

As a PM intern on Microsoft Security's eDiscovery team, I led research with users and engineers, prototyped the fix, and A/B tested a redesign that cut query errors sharply.

Role
Explore PM Intern
Company
Microsoft Security (eDiscovery)
When
Summer 2020
Skills
User research, prototyping, A/B testing

The problem

Microsoft Security's eDiscovery experience relied on KQL queries to retrieve electronically stored information for legal and investigative use. Users were making frequent syntax errors, and the existing query builder did not catch them early, so people built queries that failed only after they had invested effort in them.

My task

As a six-week PM intern, improve the query-building experience. The goal was concrete: help users build correct queries more often.

What I did

  • Led discussion sessions with users, managers, and engineers to identify the specific error cases people hit and the remediation behaviors that helped them recover.
  • Built Figma prototypes of a query-building experience designed to catch syntax and structural errors earlier and give users more visibility and control.
  • Designed an A/B test comparing the new design against the existing experience, so the improvement could be measured rather than asserted.
  • Implemented part of the design when I moved to the SWE half of the internship, shipping the redesign rather than handing off a deck.
74%

Improvement in user query-building correctness through the new design, measured via A/B test against the existing experience.

Why it matters for product

This was my first taste of the PM loop done properly: research the real failure modes, prototype a specific fix, prove it with an experiment, then help ship it. It is the earliest signal of the instincts I have been deepening ever since, and it is the through-line from a 2020 internship to the founder work I do now. The 74% is real, measured, and mine.
