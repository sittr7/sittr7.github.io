## Selected work

Each title links to the full case study.

### [The 12-Team Purview PRD](https://sittr7.github.io/case-studies/purview-prd.html)
*Cross-functional, technical.* Purview's policy sync logic had drifted into 12 different shapes across 12 product teams. From an engineering seat with no formal PM authority, I wrote and aligned a single PRD to standardize it. **Result: 12 teams committed to one shared spec.**

### [30-Person Usability Study](https://sittr7.github.io/case-studies/usability-study.html)
*User research, marketplace.* A mixed-method study that broke abstract donor-priority metrics into measurable sub-components, turning "we think this is good UX" into a defensible product-decision framework. **Result: abstract metrics became a measurable framework.**

### [From Donor Research to OKRs](https://sittr7.github.io/case-studies/donor-okrs.html)
*Research, OKRs.* Before writing any code, I ran a 24-person donor-motivation study to learn what donors actually optimize for. **Result: three success metrics (ease of use, convenience, cost) that drove every product decision.**

### [Lumen Impact Founding PRD](https://sittr7.github.io/case-studies/lumen-prd.html)
*Scoping, roadmap, KPIs.* Turned a business idea into an executable plan: MVP scoped across 11 feature workstreams, KPIs defined, personas built from competitive analysis, and a phased roadmap committed. **Result: a single source of truth for the MVP.**

### [KQL Query Builder Redesign](https://sittr7.github.io/case-studies/kql-redesign.html)
*Early PM, A/B testing.* As a PM intern on Microsoft Security's eDiscovery team, I led research with users and engineers, prototyped in Figma, and A/B tested a redesign. **Result: 74% improvement in query correctness.**
